What is EMI in a Home Loan?

EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment) is the fixed monthly payment a borrower makes to repay a home loan in India. Each EMI consists of a principal component and an interest component, calculated using the formula:
EMI = P × r × (1+r)ⁿ / ((1+r)ⁿ - 1) Where P is principal, r is monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and n is the number of monthly instalments.

How Does the RBI Repo Rate Affect Your Home Loan?

The Reserve Bank of India's repo rate (currently 6.50%) is the rate at which RBI lends to commercial banks. Banks set their home loan rates as "Repo Rate + Spread." A 0.25% cut in the repo rate typically reduces floating rate home loan EMIs by approximately ₹160–₹200 per lakh of loan outstanding.

Fixed vs Floating Rate Home Loans in India

Most home loans in India are floating rate, linked to the bank's RLLR (Repo Linked Lending Rate). These change when the RBI adjusts the repo rate. Fixed rate home loans are less common and carry a premium of 1–2% over floating rates but protect you from rate increases during your fixed period.

Income Tax Benefits on Home Loans

  • Section 24(b): Deduction up to ₹2 lakh per year on interest paid for a self-occupied property under the old tax regime.
  • Section 80C: Deduction up to ₹1.5 lakh per year on principal repayment, along with other 80C investments.
  • Section 80EEA: Additional ₹1.5 lakh deduction for first-time home buyers on affordable housing (stamp duty value ≤ ₹45 lakh).

India Home Loan FAQs

  • EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment) is the fixed monthly payment a borrower makes to repay a home loan in India. Each EMI consists of a principal component and an interest component, calculated using the formula EMI = P × r × (1+r)ⁿ / ((1+r)ⁿ - 1), where P is the loan principal, r is the monthly interest rate, and n is the number of monthly instalments.

  • The Reserve Bank of India's (RBI) repo rate is currently 6.50% as of June 2026. This rate directly influences home loan interest rates offered by banks like SBI, HDFC, and ICICI, with most floating-rate home loans priced at repo rate plus a spread. When RBI increases the repo rate, floating home loan EMIs rise; when it cuts, EMIs fall.

  • On a ₹50,000 per month salary in India, most banks apply the FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio) of 40–50%, allowing approximately ₹20,000–₹25,000 in monthly EMI. At SBI's current rate of 8.50% for 20 years, this translates to a home loan eligibility of approximately ₹23–₹29 lakh. Loan eligibility increases significantly with a co-applicant (spouse or parent) and reduces for those with existing EMI obligations.

  • Yes, home loan interest is tax-deductible in India under Section 24(b) of the Income Tax Act, up to ₹2 lakh per year for a self-occupied property under the old tax regime. The principal repayment is deductible up to ₹1.5 lakh per year under Section 80C. First-time home buyers purchasing affordable housing may claim an additional ₹1.5 lakh under Section 80EEA. These deductions are not available under the new default tax regime.

  • The minimum down payment for a home loan in India is 10% for properties valued up to ₹30 lakh, 20% for properties between ₹30–₹75 lakh, and 25% for properties above ₹75 lakh, as per RBI guidelines on Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratios. A larger down payment reduces your EMI burden and total interest cost significantly.
